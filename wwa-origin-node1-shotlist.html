Full Script
Read in full before shooting — use teleprompter ▼
A lot of driven men — and I mean most of them — burn out in silence.

And it's just how we're wired.

We were taught that the grind is the goal. That any moment of rest equates to failure.

That belief is suffocating.

Nobody tells you that's how you disappear.

You don't wake up one day at the bottom. You just look up and realize you've been there for a while.

I was angry at everything. Exhausted. Isolated in a city across the country from my family and support system.

There was no home.

"It's only temporary." It never was.

That's not drive. That's a slow collapse dressed up as ambition.

And in the midst of collapse is where Waterworld begins. Not in the gym. In the wreckage.

The one thing that makes or breaks this shoot: the pauses. This script is not meant to be delivered fast. Every line needs space after it. The silence is not dead air — it's the emotion landing. If Ash rushes through this, the intimacy collapses. Slow is the instruction.

Teleprompter note: the teleprompter must be positioned directly behind or beside the lens so eye contact reads as direct to camera. If the eyes track left or right to read, reshoot. The eye contact is the entire hook.

Series note: this setting, this frame, this wardrobe aesthetic is locked for all 6 nodes. Do not change the location between nodes. The visual consistency is what tells the viewer this is a body of work, not six separate videos.

Editing Notes
Edit
No cuts in the primary take
The entire video is one continuous shot of Ash. No jump cuts, no angle changes, no cutaways except the single B-roll drop on the final line. The rawness of one unbroken take is the format. Any cut breaks the intimacy.
Edit
Text overlay — each line as it's spoken, below chest
Clean minimal font, white, below the chest line. One line at a time. Appears as Ash speaks it. Never two lines simultaneously. This captures the muted viewer without breaking the visual for sound-on viewers.
Edit
Music — ambient, melancholic, barely audible
If music is used, it sits underneath — not driving the pace, just creating atmosphere. Slow, minimal, emotional. It should not be identifiable as a track. If it calls attention to itself, remove it. The voice is the only thing that matters.
Edit
Grade — consistent across all 6 nodes
Establish the grade for this node and lock it as the series grade. Every Origin node gets the same colour treatment. Slightly cooler, slightly darker than standard WWA palette. This is a different emotional register to the Contrast content — the grade should reflect that.
