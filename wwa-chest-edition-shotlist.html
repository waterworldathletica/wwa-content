Camera setup — critical: 16mm DZO lens. Camera set far enough back that full body is visible on each half of the split frame. Vertical format. Both sides shot from the exact same distance and angle so the split reads clean in the edit. Bad form needs to be exaggerated enough to be readable on a small screen — subtle differences disappear in a split frame.

Editing Notes
Edit
Split screen — persistent labels throughout
"Most People" label top left. "WWA" label top right. Both visible from the first frame to the last. Clean minimal font, white. Never remove the labels mid-video.
Edit
Hook interruption — cut before the rep completes
The flash preview cuts away before the first rep is finished. Leave them mid-movement. That's what creates the pull-back into the video after the hook line lands.
Edit
Music — drives the pacing, no voiceover after hook
Everything after the hook line is silent except music. Pick a track with a strong beat — cut each exercise change to the beat. The rhythm is what keeps people watching through five exercises.
Edit
Grade — match both sides exactly
Both sides of the split must be colour-graded identically. WWA palette — crushed blacks, cooler midtones. If the two sides look different, the split reads as two different videos. They need to feel like one frame.
