Most asked question I get.
"What's the core routine?" "How do you train your abs?"
I don't.

There is no ab routine.
No designated time for crunches, sit-ups,
or flopping around on the ground like a fucking fish.

I had Eight Minute Abs downloaded on my phone.
That's exactly when I had nothing to show for it.

Now I don't specifically train my abs at all.
My core is insanely shredded.

That's not a flex.
That's a data point. And there's a reason for it.

Your core is not a muscle group you switch on
the second your back hits the floor.
It's your equalizer.
The foundation of every movement you perform.
Mine never turns off.

Motion doesn't mean progress.
Precision does.

The six pack was never the focus.
But that's what technical training does.

Editing Notes
Edit
Text overlay — every line on screen as it's spoken
One line at a time. Clean minimal font, white or off-white. Never two lines simultaneously. This is the hook for muted viewers — the text has to carry the script alone.
Edit
Comedy timing — do not cut away from Ash during the fish
Both Ash and Henry must be in frame simultaneously. The deadpan only works if you see Ash not reacting. Cutting to Henry alone kills the joke. Ash foreground, Henry background, both visible.
Edit
Hold after "Mine never turns off" — do not cut
1–2 seconds of silence after delivery before moving to Shot 6. The pause is the punctuation. Filling it immediately kills the line.
Edit
Grade — WWA palette, cool and sharp
Gym lighting runs warm. Correct it. Crushed blacks, cooler midtones. The physique should read defined and sharp — not washed out, not orange. Consistent with WWA palette across all shots.
