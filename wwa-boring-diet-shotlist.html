Location Strategy — Grocery Store
Primary rig: Sony FX3 on gimbal — smooth movement through aisles, cinematic, looks intentional. Henry operates gimbal moving alongside Ash. Upper body portrait frame throughout.

Backup rig: FX3 handheld — if gimbal creates too large a footprint in a tighter store. Same framing, slightly more raw energy.

Store selection: Avoid large mainstream chains with strict security. Target independent or local grocery stores, ethnic grocery stores, or smaller food markets where security presence is minimal. All that's needed is shelves with food.

Approach: Run and gun. No lighting setup, no tripod, no footprint. Quick in, quick out. If questioned, move on and find another location.

Aisle sequencing: Produce → Cereal/Snacks → Beverages → back to Produce or Meat. The environment indicts itself — Ash doesn't have to point at anything.

I grew up in that system too. Same cereal. Same processed lunch. Same food as comfort cycle. And I paid for it with my health, my energy, and my body.

The rewire wasn't easy. But it was simple.

Eat what your body recognizes. Cut what it doesn't.

Editing Notes
Edit
Aisle transitions — smooth cuts between sections, no jump cuts
Cut between aisle sections between lines — never mid-sentence. The transitions should feel like chapters, not interruptions. Each new aisle is a new visual beat matching the content shift.
Edit
Text overlays — key lines only, high contrast, bold
"Limited ingredients. Boring." — text overlay, slightly mocking tone in the font weight. "I'd rather be boring than broken." — large, bold, centered. These two lines get the most prominent text treatment in the video. Everything else is standard below-chest text overlay.
Edit
Grade — natural, slightly cooler. Not cinematic dark like gym content
This is a different environment from the gym shoots. Grade should feel real and slightly documentary — natural lighting of the grocery store, slightly cooled. Not the dark moody WWA gym grade. The rawness of the environment is part of the visual argument.
