Full Script
Read in full before shooting — use teleprompter ▼
Depression has a body.

It used to be mine.

Skinny fat. Soft. It was easy for me to disappear.

I spent fifteen years in nonstop daily pain.

Excruciating lower back pain. An underdeveloped core. Weak glutes.

I was in my twenties and walking a city block was a problem.

I've seen every lower back stretch on YouTube. That was my daily routine.

I was desperate. I spent years hunting for just one second of peace — which never came.

Because nothing helps when the days you're living are the problem.

I made peace with pain. Told myself these were just the cards I was dealt.

The difference between then and now isn't motion. It's direction.

You're not broken. You just need to learn how to build.

B-Roll Placement
Drop after the final line. No voiceover. No text. Just the physique. This is the most powerful frame in the entire node — do not undercut it with anything.

The most important line in this node: "I was in my twenties and walking a city block was a problem." That's the scroll-stop. Someone in their twenties or thirties reading that either recognises themselves or recognises someone they love. Deliver it flat. No drama. The flatness is what makes it devastating.

The closing B-roll is the entire point of this node. Node 2 exists to show the before so the after lands. The physique shot at the end is not vanity — it's the payoff for everything that came before it. It needs to be captured clean, pumped, defined. The contrast with everything described in the script is the hook into Node 3.
