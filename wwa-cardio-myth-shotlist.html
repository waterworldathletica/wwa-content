Caption
Most people don't realize they're sabotaging their own session before it even starts.

Common misconception: cardio burns more calories, so more cardio equals more fat loss.

Reality: cardio burns calories and muscle equally. Over time that leaves you smaller but soft — not lean and defined.

Lifting reshapes the body. Cardio just shrinks it.

Build mode over burn mode. Every time.

Full Script ▼
If you start your workout with cardio, you're blocking your ability to build muscle.

Your body has two modes — build mode, which is lifting, and burn mode, which is cardio.

In build mode, your body releases a protein called mTOR — the signal to build and repair muscle.

In burn mode, your body releases an enzyme called AMPK — the signal to break down fuel and burn energy.

Now here's your problem.

Every time you do cardio before lifting, you've activated the release of AMPK — which actively shuts down the release of mTOR.

This means you've blocked your ability to build before you've touched a single weight.

It's like shooting yourself in the foot before the race even starts.

Cardio burns through your primary fuel levels — which means by the time you even begin lifting, you're already running on fumes.

You're not undertrained. You're misdirected.

Skinny fat is cardio's greatest achievement.

One hour. Six days a week. Zero treadmill.

And before you blame genetics — you're looking at the product of someone who's not supposed to be here.
