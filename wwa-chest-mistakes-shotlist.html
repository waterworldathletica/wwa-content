The one thing that makes or breaks this shoot: Henry's bad form has to be genuinely wrong — not subtly off. Exaggerated enough to be readable immediately. And Ash's corrections have to be hands-on, visible, real. The coaching relationship on camera is the authority. If it looks staged it loses everything.

Editing Notes
Edit
Hook to corrections — hard cut, no transition
The hook line ends. Hard cut straight into the first correction. No fade, no wipe. The abruptness is intentional — it signals the video is shifting gears and keeps the viewer engaged.
Edit
Text overlays — ❌ red, ✅ green. One at a time.
Every mistake and correction labelled on screen. Red X for the mistake, green tick for the correction. Clean minimal font. Text appears as the action happens — not before. Never two labels simultaneously.
Edit
Closing shots — no text, no cut
Wide physique shot holds for 2–3 seconds. Then punch in to the chest. No text on either closing shot. No music change. The silence and stillness after all the corrections is what makes these frames hit. Don't fill them.
Edit
Grade — WWA palette throughout
Crushed blacks, cooler midtones. Gym lighting runs warm — correct it. The physique shots at the end especially need to be sharp and defined. That's the proof frame. It can't look washed out.
