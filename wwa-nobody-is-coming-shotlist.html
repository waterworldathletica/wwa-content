Before You Shoot
Pre-shoot
Find the secluded corner
Back against dumbbell rack or isolated wall. Away from foot traffic. The environment needs to feel contained, like the viewer stumbled on it. Not a stage.
Pre-shoot
FX3 — low angle, slightly unconventional
Floor level or just below eye line. Face and chest in frame. Loose, slightly off-center. Gym equipment visible behind but not distracting. No ring light — existing gym light only.

Editing Notes
Edit
No cuts. Single continuous take.
The rawness lives in the unbroken feel. If a cut is needed make it invisible, same frame, different moment. No B-roll. No transitions.
Edit
Grade: desaturated, cooler tones, crushed blacks
Match WWA palette. No warm film look. Gym light should feel grey and real, not cinematic. Rawness of image reinforces the message.
Edit
Captions: one line at a time, minimal font
White or off-white. Never two sentences on screen at once. Each line its own beat. NOBODY IS COMING overlay must appear at 0:00.
